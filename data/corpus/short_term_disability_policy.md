# Short-Term Disability Policy

**Effective Date:** January 10, 2023
**Applies to:** All benefits-eligible Acme Corp employees

## 1. Benefit Summary

Employees who are unable to work due to a qualifying medical condition receive short-term disability (STD) benefits of 60% of base salary for up to 12 weeks, following a 7-day waiting period. Benefits are paid biweekly through standard payroll.

The 7-day waiting period begins on the first day the employee is unable to work. Employees may use accrued sick leave or vacation to cover the waiting period.

## 2. Eligibility

All benefits-eligible employees (full-time and part-time working 20+ hours per week) are eligible for STD from their date of hire. There is no tenure requirement.

## 3. Qualifying Conditions

STD covers the employee's own medical condition that prevents them from performing the essential functions of their job. Examples include:

- Surgery and post-surgical recovery.
- Serious illness or injury.
- Pregnancy-related medical complications (pre- and post-delivery).
- Mental health conditions requiring inpatient treatment or intensive outpatient programs.

STD does not cover absences related to caring for a family member. Employees needing leave to care for others should refer to the FMLA Leave Procedure.

## 4. Claims Process

1. **Notify HR** of the expected absence as soon as practicable.
2. **Submit a claim form** and medical documentation from the treating provider within 10 calendar days of the first day of disability. The documentation must include diagnosis, treatment plan, expected duration, and a statement that the employee is unable to perform their job duties.
3. **HR and the STD administrator** review the claim and issue a determination within 5 business days.
4. If approved, benefits begin after the 7-day waiting period and continue for the duration certified by the provider, up to the 12-week maximum.

Employees must submit updated medical documentation every 30 days for ongoing claims.

## 5. Coordination with Other Benefits

STD benefits coordinate with other Acme Corp leave programs:

- **FMLA:** STD pay often runs concurrently with FMLA leave. FMLA provides unpaid job protection; STD provides income replacement. An employee with a qualifying condition typically receives both: job protection from FMLA and 60% pay from STD.
- **Parental leave:** Birthing parents with pregnancy-related complications may receive STD benefits during the medical recovery portion of their leave, while parental leave pay (100% of salary) covers the bonding portion.
- **Vacation/sick leave:** Employees may supplement STD pay with accrued vacation or sick leave to reach up to 100% of base salary. This is optional.

## 6. How STD Differs from FMLA

| | Short-Term Disability | FMLA |
|---|---|---|
| **What it provides** | Income replacement (60% of salary) | Unpaid job protection |
| **Duration** | Up to 12 weeks after 7-day waiting period | Up to 12 weeks per 12-month period |
| **Eligibility** | All benefits-eligible employees from hire | 12 months of tenure required |
| **Covers family care** | No — employee's own condition only | Yes — employee or qualifying family member |
| **Job protection** | No independent protection | Yes — same or equivalent role guaranteed |

In practice, most employees who qualify for STD also qualify for concurrent FMLA leave, receiving both income replacement and job protection.

## 7. Return to Work

Employees must obtain a fitness-for-duty certification from their treating provider before returning. The certification must confirm the employee can perform the essential functions of their role, with or without reasonable accommodations.

If the employee requires accommodations upon return, Human Resources will coordinate with the employee and their manager through the interactive accommodation process.

## 8. Policy Governance

This policy is administered by Human Resources in partnership with the company's STD administrator. Questions should be directed to hr@acmecorp.com.
