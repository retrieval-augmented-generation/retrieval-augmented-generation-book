# Quarterly Compliance Reporting Schedule

**Fiscal Year:** 2025
**Maintained by:** Office of the Chief Compliance Officer

## 1. Reporting Deadlines

| Quarter | Period Covered | Report Due Date | Board Review |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 | January 1 – March 31 | April 30 | May board meeting |
| Q2 | April 1 – June 30 | July 31 | August board meeting |
| Q3 | July 1 – September 30 | October 31 | November board meeting |
| Q4 | October 1 – December 31 | January 31 | February board meeting |

All reports must be submitted by 5:00 PM PT on the due date. Late submissions are escalated per Section 4.

## 2. Responsible Departments

Each department is responsible for submitting its section of the quarterly compliance report:

| Department | Report Section | Submits To |
|---|---|---|
| Information Security | Security incidents, vulnerability metrics, patch compliance, access review completion | Chief Compliance Officer |
| Engineering | SOC 2 control evidence, change management logs, deployment audit trail | Chief Compliance Officer |
| Finance | SOX control testing results, revenue recognition compliance, expense audit samples | Chief Compliance Officer |
| Human Resources | Training completion rates, harassment report summary (anonymized), policy acknowledgment rates | Chief Compliance Officer |
| Legal | Regulatory change log, litigation updates, contract compliance summary | Chief Compliance Officer |
| Customer Success | SLA performance, customer data subject requests processed, breach notifications sent | Chief Compliance Officer |

The Chief Compliance Officer (Jordan Patel) consolidates all department submissions into the company-wide Quarterly Compliance Report for board presentation.

## 3. Report Contents

Each quarterly compliance report includes:

- **Executive summary:** Overall compliance posture and key changes from the prior quarter.
- **Incident summary:** Number and severity of compliance-related incidents, with root cause and remediation status.
- **Metrics dashboard:** Training completion percentage, policy acknowledgment rate, open audit findings, mean time to remediate compliance issues.
- **Regulatory update:** New or changed regulations affecting Acme Corp, with impact assessment and action plan.
- **Exceptions and waivers:** Active security or policy exceptions, with justification and review dates.
- **Upcoming deadlines:** External audit dates, certification renewals, and regulatory filing deadlines for the next quarter.

## 4. Escalation for Late Submissions

If a department does not submit its report section by the due date:

| Days Late | Action |
|---|---|
| 1–3 days | Automated reminder to the department head from the compliance tracking system |
| 4–7 days | Chief Compliance Officer contacts the department head directly |
| 8+ days | Escalated to the CEO; department head must provide a written explanation and remediation timeline |

Late submissions are noted in the board report. Repeated late submissions (2 or more consecutive quarters) trigger a compliance process review for the responsible department.

## 5. Policy Governance

This schedule is maintained by the Office of the Chief Compliance Officer. Questions should be directed to compliance@acmecorp.com.
