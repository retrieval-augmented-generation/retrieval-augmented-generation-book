# Vacation Policy

**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024
**Applies to:** All full-time Acme Corp employees

## 1. Accrual Schedule

Full-time employees accrue paid vacation based on continuous years of service, measured from date of hire:

| Tenure | Annual Vacation Days | Annual Hours | Per Pay Period (Biweekly) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | 15 days | 120 hours | 4.62 hours |
| Years 2–9 | 20 days | 160 hours | 6.15 hours |
| 10+ years | 25 days | 200 hours | 7.69 hours |

Accrual begins on the employee's first day of employment. Tier increases take effect on the anniversary of the hire date.

Part-time employees accrue vacation on a prorated basis relative to their scheduled weekly hours.

## 2. Carryover

Employees may carry over a maximum of 40 hours of unused vacation into the following calendar year. Any balance exceeding 40 hours on January 1 is forfeited. Acme Corp does not pay out forfeited vacation time.

Employees are encouraged to use their full vacation allotment each year. Managers should work with their teams to ensure vacation is scheduled throughout the year rather than concentrated in Q4.

## 3. Requesting Vacation

- Requests of fewer than 5 consecutive days require manager approval at least 5 business days in advance.
- Requests of 5 or more consecutive days require manager approval at least 2 weeks in advance.
- Managers may deny or request rescheduling of vacation based on business needs, project deadlines, or team coverage requirements.

All requests are submitted through the HR portal. Approved vacation is reflected on the team calendar automatically.

## 4. Blackout Periods

Certain teams may designate blackout periods during which vacation requests are restricted. Common blackout periods include:

- **Engineering:** The two weeks surrounding a major product release, as designated by the VP of Engineering.
- **Finance:** The last 5 business days of each fiscal quarter (March, June, September, December) and the first 10 business days of January for year-end close.
- **Customer Success:** The week of annual contract renewals for assigned accounts.

Blackout periods are published at least 30 days in advance. Exceptions require written approval from the department head.

## 5. Vacation During Probationary Period

Employees in their 90-day probationary period accrue vacation from day one but may not use accrued vacation during the first 60 days of employment except in emergencies approved by Human Resources.

## 6. Separation

Upon voluntary resignation or involuntary termination, accrued and unused vacation is paid out at the employee's current base rate in the final paycheck, in accordance with applicable state law. Forfeited carryover balances (per Section 2) are not eligible for payout.

## 7. Policy Governance

This policy is administered by Human Resources. Questions should be directed to hr@acmecorp.com. This policy is reviewed annually and was last updated on March 1, 2024.
