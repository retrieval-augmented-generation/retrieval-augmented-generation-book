# Data Protection Officer — Role Description

**Effective Date:** December 1, 2024
**Current DPO:** Mariana Costa
**Applies to:** Acme Corp organization-wide

---

## 1. Purpose

The Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for overseeing Acme Corp's data protection strategy and ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws, including GDPR, HIPAA, and state-level privacy regulations. The role was established to meet the requirements of GDPR Article 37, which mandates the designation of a DPO for organizations that carry out large-scale processing of personal data.

## 2. Scope

The DPO's mandate covers:

- All personal data processed by Acme Corp, whether as a data controller (employee data, prospect data) or as a data processor (customer data processed on behalf of enterprise customers under a Data Processing Agreement).
- All Acme Corp systems that store or process personal data, across all regions (US-East, EU-West).
- All departments that handle personal data, including Engineering, Customer Success, Sales, Marketing, and Human Resources.

## 3. Responsibilities

### 3.1 Advisory

- Advise the company on its obligations under data protection law.
- Provide guidance on data protection impact assessments (DPIAs) for new products, features, or processing activities that involve personal data.
- Review and approve data retention schedules, deletion procedures, and anonymization processes. The DPO is the designated owner of the Data Retention Policy.
- Advise on data transfer mechanisms for international data flows (Standard Contractual Clauses, Binding Corporate Rules).

### 3.2 Monitoring and Audit

- Monitor compliance with GDPR, HIPAA, and internal data protection policies.
- Conduct or commission annual data protection audits.
- Review the data processing register and ensure it is complete and current.
- Verify deletions executed under the Data Retention Policy and the Customer Deletion Workflow.

### 3.3 Data Subject Rights

- Oversee the process for responding to data subject access requests (DSARs), erasure requests, and portability requests.
- Ensure that responses are issued within the legally required timeframe (30 days under GDPR, with a possible 60-day extension for complex requests).

### 3.4 Breach Response

- Serve as the internal point of contact for data breach assessment and notification.
- Determine whether a breach meets the threshold for notification to supervisory authorities (GDPR: 72 hours) and affected individuals.
- Coordinate with the Chief Compliance Officer and Legal on regulatory notifications.

### 3.5 Training

- Develop and maintain the data privacy training curriculum for all employees.
- Ensure that employees who handle personal data complete annual data privacy training per the Mandatory Training Catalog.
- Deliver specialized training for teams with elevated data access (Engineering, Customer Success, Sales).

## 4. Reporting Line

The DPO reports directly to the Board of Directors through the Audit Committee. This reporting line ensures independence from operational management, as required by GDPR Article 38.

The DPO also has a working relationship with:

- The Chief Compliance Officer (Jordan Patel), for coordination on regulatory matters.
- The VP of Engineering (Sarah Chen), for coordination on technical data protection measures.
- The General Counsel, for coordination on legal matters and data subject litigation.

The DPO cannot be dismissed or penalized for performing their duties. Any proposed changes to the DPO role must be reviewed by the Audit Committee.

## 5. Qualifications and Certifications

The DPO must hold:

- A law degree, privacy certification, or equivalent professional qualification in data protection.
- At least one of the following certifications: CIPP/E (Certified Information Privacy Professional — Europe), CIPM (Certified Information Privacy Manager), or CDPSE (Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer).

The current DPO, Mariana Costa, holds a Master of Laws (LLM) in Information Technology and Privacy Law from Tilburg University and is certified as CIPP/E and CIPM.

## 6. Ongoing Professional Development

The DPO is required to:

- Complete at least 20 hours of continuing privacy education per year.
- Attend at least one data protection conference or regulatory workshop annually.
- Maintain active certification status with the IAPP (International Association of Privacy Professionals).

Training costs are funded by the Legal department budget and are not subject to the standard employee tuition reimbursement cap.

## 7. Resources

The DPO has authority to:

- Access all data processing activities and all personal data held by Acme Corp, without restriction.
- Engage external legal counsel or specialist consultants for complex data protection matters, with budget approval from the General Counsel.
- Escalate concerns directly to the Board of Directors if internal remediation is insufficient.

The DPO is supported by one Privacy Analyst who assists with DSAR processing, audit coordination, and register maintenance.

## 8. Contact

The DPO can be reached at dpo@acmecorp.com. For general privacy questions, employees should contact privacy@acmecorp.com.
