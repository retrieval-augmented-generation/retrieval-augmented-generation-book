MFA Enrollment Guide

Last Updated: April 15, 2024
Applies to: All Acme Corp employees and contractors

1. Why MFA Is Required

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all Acme Corp accounts. MFA protects your account by requiring a second verification method in addition to your password. It is mandatory for VPN access, cloud applications, production systems, and any system handling Confidential or Restricted data.

2. Supported Factors

MethodRecommendedNotes
Hardware security key (YubiKey, Titan)Yes — preferredMost secure; phishing-resistant. Acme Corp provides one YubiKey per employee on request.
Authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password)YesTOTP-based; works offline. Use this if you do not have a hardware key.
Push notification (Okta Verify)AcceptableConvenient but susceptible to push fatigue attacks. Do not approve prompts you did not initiate.
SMSNot permittedSMS is not an approved MFA method due to SIM-swapping and interception vulnerabilities.

3. Enrollment Steps

  1. Navigate to sso.acmecorp.com/settings/mfa and sign in with your Acme Corp credentials.
  2. Click Add Authentication Method.
  3. Select your preferred method:
    • Hardware key: Insert your security key and follow the browser prompt to register it.
    • Authenticator app: Scan the QR code displayed on screen with your authenticator app. Enter the 6-digit code to confirm.
    • Push notification: Install Okta Verify on your phone, scan the QR code, and approve the test push.
  4. Enroll at least two methods (one primary, one backup). This ensures you can still log in if your primary method is unavailable.
  5. Generate and save your recovery codes (see Section 4).

4. Recovery Codes

During enrollment, you will be given 10 one-time recovery codes. Each code can be used once to log in if all your MFA methods are unavailable (e.g., lost phone and security key).

Important: Store your recovery codes in a secure location — a password manager or a sealed envelope in a physically secure place. Do not store them in a plaintext file on your computer or in an unencrypted note on your phone.

If you exhaust all recovery codes or lose access to them, contact IT at it-support@acmecorp.com for an identity-verified account recovery. This process requires in-person identity verification or a video call with a government-issued ID.

5. Troubleshooting

IssueResolution
Authenticator code not acceptedEnsure your phone's clock is synced automatically (Settings > Date & Time > Automatic). TOTP codes are time-sensitive.
Hardware key not recognizedTry a different USB port. Ensure your browser supports WebAuthn (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari all do).
Lost phone with authenticator appUse your backup MFA method or a recovery code. Then re-enroll a new device.
Lost security keyUse your backup method to log in, then remove the lost key from your account and enroll a replacement.
All methods unavailableContact IT for identity-verified account recovery.
