# HR Data Retention Schedule

**Effective Date:** June 1, 2024
**Approved by:** General Counsel, Data Protection Officer (Mariana Costa)
**Applies to:** All HR records maintained by Acme Corp, regardless of storage format or location

---

## 1. Purpose

This schedule defines the retention periods for HR-related data. It supplements the company-wide Data Retention Policy with HR-specific requirements driven by employment law, tax regulations, and operational needs. Where this schedule specifies a longer or shorter retention period than the general policy, this schedule controls for HR records.

## 2. Retention Periods

| Record Type | Retention Period | Regulatory Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Job applications (hired candidates) | 2 years from hire date | EEOC, OFCCP |
| Job applications (not hired) | 2 years from application date | EEOC, OFCCP |
| Employee personnel files | Duration of employment + 7 years | Federal and state employment law |
| Payroll and tax records | 7 years from date of record | IRS (§6001), SOX |
| I-9 employment verification | 3 years from hire date or 1 year after termination, whichever is later | USCIS |
| Benefits enrollment records | Duration of enrollment + 7 years | ERISA |
| Training completion records | 5 years from date of completion | SOC 2 evidence, OSHA (for safety training) |
| Performance reviews | 7 years from review date | Internal policy, litigation hold readiness |
| Disciplinary records | Duration of employment + 7 years | Federal and state employment law |
| Workers' compensation claims | 7 years from claim closure | State law (varies) |
| FMLA records | 3 years from leave end date | DOL (29 CFR §825.500) |
| Exit interviews | 3 years from separation date | Internal policy |
| Background check results | 2 years from hire date | FCRA |
| EEO-1 reports | 3 years | EEOC |
| OSHA injury/illness logs | 5 years from end of calendar year | OSHA (29 CFR §1904.33) |

## 3. EU Employee Records

For employees based in Acme Corp's EU offices or EU-based remote employees, additional constraints apply per GDPR and local labor law:

- Personal identification data follows the Data Retention Policy's EU-Specific Requirements: deleted or anonymized within 3 years of the end of the employment relationship unless a specific legal basis for longer retention exists.
- Payroll and tax records may be retained for the full 7 years where required by local tax law (e.g., German tax law requires 10 years for certain financial records — the longer local requirement applies).
- Performance reviews for EU employees are retained for 3 years after separation, not 7, unless a legal hold is in effect.

The Data Protection Officer reviews EU HR retention schedules annually to ensure alignment with local requirements in each EU jurisdiction where Acme Corp has employees.

## 4. Data Minimization

HR must periodically review retained records to ensure that only the minimum necessary data is kept:

- Sensitive personal data (medical information, disability documentation, background check details) that is no longer needed for its original purpose should be deleted even if the broader employee file is still within its retention period.
- Interview notes and scoring rubrics for candidates not hired should be limited to the standardized evaluation form. Informal notes should be deleted after the hiring decision is finalized.

## 5. Deletion Process

When a record reaches the end of its retention period:

1. The HR system generates a deletion queue during the first week of each quarter.
2. The HR Records Coordinator reviews the queue and confirms that no legal hold or active investigation applies.
3. Electronic records are deleted from the HRIS and any backup systems per the Data Retention Policy's Data Deletion Procedures.
4. Physical records (if any) are shredded using a cross-cut shredder or a certified document destruction vendor.
5. The Data Protection Officer certifies the deletion.

## 6. Legal Holds

If a legal hold is issued by the General Counsel, all HR records within the scope of the hold are preserved regardless of the retention schedule. Legal holds override this schedule until released. See the Data Retention Policy for the legal hold process.

## 7. Policy Governance

This schedule is maintained by Human Resources in coordination with Legal and the Data Protection Officer (Mariana Costa). It is reviewed annually. Questions should be directed to hr@acmecorp.com.
