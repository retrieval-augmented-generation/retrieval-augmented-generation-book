# Compliance Violations Log — 2024

**Reporting Period:** January 1 – December 31, 2024
**Prepared by:** Internal Audit team
**Approved by:** Jordan Patel, Chief Compliance Officer

---

## Summary

Seven compliance violations were recorded in 2024, down from eleven in 2023. Three of the seven violations triggered specific policy changes. The remaining four were resolved through existing disciplinary and remediation processes without requiring policy modifications.

---

## Violations and Policy Changes

### V-2024-001: Unauthorized Customer Data Export (January)

**Department:** Customer Success
**Description:** A Customer Success Manager exported a customer's usage data to a personal Google Drive account to prepare for a quarterly business review. The export contained Confidential data and violated the Data Classification Policy's prohibition on storing Confidential data on personal cloud services.

**Resolution:** Written warning issued. Employee completed a remedial data handling training session.

**Policy change triggered:** None. Existing policy was sufficient; enforcement was the gap.

---

### V-2024-002: Missed DSAR Response Deadline (March)

**Department:** Customer Success
**Description:** Three data subject access requests from EU customers were responded to in 38, 41, and 45 days — exceeding the GDPR-required 30-day window. The requests were handled by individual CSMs instead of being routed to the Data Protection Officer.

**Resolution:** DPO office assumed responsibility for all DSAR processing.

**Policy change triggered:** **Yes.** The Customer Deletion Workflow was updated (September 2024, v2.0) to add an explicit DSAR routing requirement: all data subject requests must be forwarded to dpo@acmecorp.com within 24 hours of receipt. Customer Success staff are no longer authorized to respond to DSARs independently.

---

### V-2024-003: Unencrypted Backup Tape (April)

**Department:** IT Operations
**Description:** A routine audit discovered that one backup tape from March contained database snapshots that were not encrypted at rest. The tape had been generated by a legacy backup job that predated the migration to encrypted backup infrastructure. No data breach occurred; the tape was stored in the secure offsite facility.

**Resolution:** The legacy backup job was decommissioned. The unencrypted tape was destroyed with certified media sanitization.

**Policy change triggered:** **Yes.** The Backup and Recovery Policy was updated (June 2023 → June 2024 revision pending) to require that IT Operations run a quarterly encryption verification scan of all backup media. Previously, encryption was verified only at creation time.

---

### V-2024-004: HIPAA Training Gap in Sales (June)

**Department:** Sales & Marketing
**Description:** Seven sales representatives handling healthcare prospects had not completed HIPAA Basics training as required by the Mandatory Training Catalog. Three accessed demo environments containing simulated PHI during product demonstrations. Discovered during the internal compliance audit.

**Resolution:** All seven completed training within 14 days of discovery. Demo environments were sanitized.

**Policy change triggered:** **Yes.** The Mandatory Training Catalog was updated (January 2025, v2.0) to add a prerequisite enforcement rule: healthcare vertical account assignment in the CRM system now requires confirmed HIPAA training completion. The CRM blocks assignment if the training record is not current.

---

### V-2024-005: Unauthorized Marketing Use of Customer Data (June)

**Department:** Sales & Marketing
**Description:** Two case studies published in Q2 included a customer's revenue figures without the customer's written approval for external use. The data was accurate but its disclosure was not authorized under the customer's contract.

**Resolution:** Case studies were taken down within 24 hours. Apology issued to the affected customer. No contractual penalty was assessed.

**Policy change triggered:** None. Marketing added a pre-publication approval checklist as an operational process change, but no policy language was modified.

---

### V-2024-006: Late Access Review (August)

**Department:** IT / Information Security
**Description:** The Q3 quarterly access review for the analytics platform and staging environment was completed 8 days past the 15-business-day SOC 2 control deadline. The system owner was on leave with no delegate assigned.

**Resolution:** IT implemented a backup reviewer assignment process for all quarterly access reviews.

**Policy change triggered:** None. The Access Control Policy already required quarterly reviews; the issue was operational, not policy-based.

---

### V-2024-007: Contractor Access Not Revoked on Time (October)

**Department:** Engineering
**Description:** A contractor's system access remained active for 12 days after their Statement of Work ended. The hiring manager did not notify IT of the SOW end date. No unauthorized activity was detected during the 12-day window.

**Resolution:** Access revoked immediately upon discovery. The hiring manager received a written reminder of the Contractor Access Policy offboarding requirements.

**Policy change triggered:** None. The Contractor Access Policy Section 5 already requires 5-day advance notification. Engineering Operations added an automated SOW expiration alert from the procurement system as an operational safeguard.
