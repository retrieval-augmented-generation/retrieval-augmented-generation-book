# HSA/FSA Policy

**Effective Date:** October 15, 2024
**Applies to:** All benefits-eligible Acme Corp employees

## 1. Health Savings Account (HSA)

### Eligibility

Employees enrolled in the Basic or Standard health plan are eligible to open an HSA. Employees enrolled in the Premium plan are not eligible, as that plan does not meet the IRS high-deductible health plan (HDHP) requirement.

### Contribution Limits (2025)

| Coverage Level | Employee + Employer Annual Limit |
|---|---|
| Individual | $4,300 |
| Family | $8,550 |
| Catch-up (age 55+) | Additional $1,000 |

Acme Corp contributes $500 (individual) or $1,000 (family) annually to each eligible employee's HSA in quarterly installments. This amount counts toward the IRS annual limit.

### Key Features

- Contributions are pre-tax (or tax-deductible if contributed outside payroll).
- Earnings grow tax-free.
- Withdrawals for qualified medical expenses are tax-free.
- Funds roll over indefinitely — there is no "use it or lose it" deadline.
- The account is owned by the employee and remains theirs if they leave Acme Corp.

### Qualified Expenses

HSA funds may be used for medical, dental, and vision expenses not covered by insurance, including deductibles, copays, coinsurance, prescription drugs, and certain over-the-counter items. A full list of qualified expenses is available on the benefits portal.

## 2. Flexible Spending Account (FSA)

### Eligibility

All benefits-eligible employees may open an FSA regardless of which health plan they are enrolled in. Acme Corp offers two FSA types:

- **Healthcare FSA:** For medical, dental, and vision expenses not covered by insurance. Employees enrolled in an HSA-eligible plan may only open a Limited-Purpose FSA (dental and vision expenses only).
- **Dependent Care FSA:** For eligible dependent care expenses such as daycare, preschool, and before/after-school programs.

### Contribution Limits (2025)

| FSA Type | Annual Maximum |
|---|---|
| Healthcare FSA | $3,300 |
| Limited-Purpose FSA | $3,300 |
| Dependent Care FSA | $5,000 ($2,500 if married filing separately) |

Acme Corp does not contribute to FSA accounts.

### Rollover Rules

- **Healthcare and Limited-Purpose FSA:** Up to $640 of unused funds may roll over into the next plan year. Balances above $640 remaining after the March 31 run-out period are forfeited.
- **Dependent Care FSA:** No rollover. Unused funds remaining after the March 31 run-out period are forfeited.

### Run-Out Period

Employees have until March 31 of the following plan year to submit claims for expenses incurred during the prior plan year. Claims submitted after March 31 are not eligible for reimbursement.

## 3. Enrollment

HSA and FSA elections are made during the annual open enrollment period (closes November 15) or within 30 days of a qualifying life event. HSA contribution changes may also be made at any time during the plan year.

Enrollment and contribution management are handled through the benefits portal at benefits.acmecorp.com.
