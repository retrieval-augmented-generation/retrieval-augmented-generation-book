# Engineering Hiring Rubric

**Effective Date:** August 1, 2024
**Owner:** Engineering Leadership + Talent Acquisition
**Classification:** Confidential — do not share with candidates

---

## 1. Interview Structure

All engineering candidates go through a standardized interview loop:

| Stage | Duration | Interviewer | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recruiter screen | 30 min | Talent Acquisition | Role fit, compensation expectations, timeline |
| Hiring manager screen | 45 min | Hiring manager | Background deep-dive, team fit, motivation |
| Technical interview 1 | 60 min | Engineer (L3+) | Coding and problem-solving |
| Technical interview 2 | 60 min | Engineer (L3+) | System design (L3+ candidates) or data structures (L1–L2) |
| Cross-functional interview | 45 min | Product Manager or Designer | Collaboration, communication, product thinking |
| Values interview | 30 min | Any interviewer trained on values rubric | Alignment with Acme Corp engineering values |

L6+ candidates add an architecture review session (90 min) with the VP of Engineering or a Distinguished Engineer.

## 2. Evaluation Criteria

Each interviewer scores the candidate on a 4-point scale:

| Score | Label | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 4 | Strong Hire | Clearly exceeds the bar for the level. Demonstrated exceptional skill in the focus area. |
| 3 | Hire | Meets the bar for the level. Solid performance with minor gaps that can be developed on the job. |
| 2 | Lean No Hire | Below the bar in meaningful ways. Significant concerns that coaching alone may not resolve. |
| 1 | No Hire | Well below the bar. Critical gaps in required competencies. |

## 3. Level Expectations

### L1 — Associate / Junior

- Solves well-scoped coding problems with standard data structures and algorithms.
- Writes clean, readable code with guidance.
- Communicates thought process clearly during the interview.
- No system design expectation.

### L2 — Mid-Level

- Solves moderately complex coding problems independently, including edge cases.
- Discusses tradeoffs in implementation choices (time vs space, readability vs performance).
- Demonstrates familiarity with one or more production-relevant technologies (databases, APIs, cloud services).
- Basic system design: can design a simple service with a database, API, and caching layer.

### L3 — Senior

- Solves complex coding problems efficiently and handles ambiguity well.
- System design: can design a multi-service system with appropriate data storage, caching, queueing, and failure handling.
- Demonstrates ownership mindset: considers testing, observability, and operational concerns.
- Evidence of mentoring or technical leadership.

### L4 — Staff

- All L3 expectations, plus:
- System design at scale: can reason about scalability, consistency tradeoffs, and multi-region architectures.
- Demonstrated impact across team boundaries.
- Evidence of driving technical strategy or leading a significant project end-to-end.

### L5+ — Senior Staff and Above

- All L4 expectations, plus:
- Demonstrates industry-caliber depth in one or more technical domains.
- Evidence of influencing engineering direction at the organizational level.
- Architecture review evaluates ability to assess tradeoffs across multiple systems and articulate long-term technical vision.

## 4. Calibration

After all interviews are complete:

1. The recruiter collects scorecards from all interviewers within 24 hours.
2. The hiring manager convenes a debrief meeting (30 min) with all interviewers.
3. Each interviewer shares their score and rationale. Discussion focuses on disagreements (2+ point spread between interviewers).
4. The hiring manager makes the final hire/no-hire decision. The decision is documented with rationale.
5. If the decision is borderline, the hiring manager may request one additional interview (different interviewer, different focus area).

### Calibration Rules

- A single "No Hire" (score 1) from any interviewer triggers a mandatory debrief discussion. The hiring manager must specifically address the concern before proceeding.
- Two or more "Lean No Hire" (score 2) across different focus areas is a strong signal against proceeding. The hiring manager must document specific reasons for overriding this signal.
- Interviewers must not change their score based on peer pressure during the debrief. If new information changes their assessment, they document the reason for the change.

## 5. Anti-Bias Practices

- Interview questions are drawn from a question bank maintained by the Engineering Leadership team. Interviewers may not use ad-hoc questions without prior approval.
- All interviewers complete the Interviewing and Bias Awareness training annually per the Mandatory Training Catalog.
- Candidate evaluation must reference only job-relevant criteria. Personal attributes (school attended, previous employer prestige, hobbies, family status) must not factor into the assessment.
- Scorecards are submitted independently before the debrief meeting to prevent anchoring.

## 6. Policy Governance

This rubric is maintained by the Engineering Leadership team in coordination with Talent Acquisition. Updates are reviewed semi-annually. Questions: eng-hiring@acmecorp.com.
