# Contractor Access Policy

**Effective Date:** July 1, 2023
**Applies to:** All contractors, consultants, and temporary workers engaged by Acme Corp

## 1. Contractor Classification

Contractors are individuals or firms engaged by Acme Corp under a Statement of Work (SOW) or consulting agreement. Contractors are not Acme Corp employees and are not eligible for employee benefits, equity grants, or employee-only programs (including the employee referral bonus, tuition reimbursement, and the wellness program).

Contractors must not be treated as employees for scheduling, supervision, or benefits purposes. Managers who engage contractors should consult with Human Resources and Legal to ensure proper classification.

## 2. System Access

Contractors are granted system access on a need-to-know basis, limited to the systems and data required to complete their contracted scope of work. Access is provisioned by IT upon receipt of a completed Contractor Access Request form signed by the hiring manager and the contractor's Acme Corp sponsor.

### 2.1 Permitted Access

- Project-specific code repositories and documentation.
- Communication tools (Slack, email) with a contractor-designated account suffix (@contractor.acmecorp.com).
- Cloud development and staging environments relevant to the contracted work.
- The company wiki, limited to pages explicitly shared by the project team.

### 2.2 Restricted Access

Contractors do not have access to:

- The employee benefits portal.
- HR systems (performance reviews, compensation data, org charts).
- Production databases or production infrastructure (unless explicitly approved in the SOW and by the VP of Engineering).
- The internal financial reporting system.
- Customer PII or PHI (unless the SOW includes a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement and the contractor has completed HIPAA training).

### 2.3 Access Duration

Contractor access is provisioned for the duration of the SOW, up to a maximum of 12 months. Extensions require a renewed SOW and a new access request. Access is automatically revoked on the SOW end date unless an extension is on file.

## 3. Security Requirements

Contractors must:

- Use Acme Corp-provisioned devices or devices that meet the Laptop Security Requirements (full disk encryption, endpoint agent, screen lock).
- Complete security awareness training within 5 business days of access being provisioned.
- Use the company VPN when accessing internal systems remotely.
- Comply with the IT Security Policy and the Data Classification Policy.

Contractors handling Restricted data must sign an NDA and complete additional data handling training.

## 4. Physical Access

Contractors may be issued temporary building access badges valid for the duration of the SOW. Contractors must be escorted in server rooms and data center areas. Badges must be returned on the last day of the engagement.

## 5. Offboarding

When a contractor engagement ends:

1. The hiring manager notifies IT at least 5 business days before the end date.
2. IT revokes all system access on the SOW end date.
3. The contractor returns any Acme Corp-issued equipment.
4. The hiring manager confirms that all deliverables have been received and that no company data remains on the contractor's personal devices.

## 6. Policy Governance

This policy is administered by Human Resources and IT. Questions should be directed to hr@acmecorp.com.
