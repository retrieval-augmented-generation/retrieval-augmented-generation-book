# US Office Data Retention Schedule

**Effective Date:** June 1, 2024
**Approved by:** General Counsel
**Applies to:** All data originating from or relating to Acme Corp's US operations (San Francisco headquarters, US-based remote employees, US customers on the US-East region)

---

## 1. Purpose

This schedule provides US-specific retention periods that supplement — and in some cases override — the company-wide Data Retention Policy. Where US federal or state law requires a specific retention period, this schedule reflects the applicable requirement. Where no US-specific requirement exists, the general Data Retention Policy applies.

## 2. US Retention Periods

| Data Category | US Retention Period | Basis | General Policy Period |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customer transaction records | 7 years | IRS §6001, SOX | 7 years (same) |
| Customer account data (active) | Duration of relationship | Operational | Duration of relationship (same) |
| Customer account data (post-termination) | Deleted within 90 days | Customer Deletion Workflow | 90 days (same) |
| Customer personal identification data | 7 years | No US storage limitation equivalent to GDPR | 3 years (EU) / 7 years (general) |
| Employee personnel files | Employment + 7 years | Federal and state employment law | Employment + 7 years (same) |
| Employee payroll and tax records | 7 years | IRS, state tax agencies | 7 years (same) |
| Job applications | 2 years | EEOC, OFCCP | 2 years (same) |
| Marketing consent records | Duration of consent + 3 years | CAN-SPAM, CCPA | Duration + 3 years (same) |
| Support ticket content | 5 years | Internal policy | 3 years (EU) |
| Audit logs | 1 year hot + 6 years cold | SOC 2, HIPAA | 1 year + 6 years (same) |

## 3. Key Differences from EU Retention

The most significant difference between US and EU retention is the absence of a storage limitation principle in US law equivalent to GDPR Article 5(1)(e). In practice, this means:

- **Customer personal identification data** (name, email, phone) may be retained for the full 7-year general retention period in the US. In the EU, this data must be deleted or anonymized within 3 years of the last processing activity for its original purpose.
- **Support ticket content** is retained for 5 years in the US (to support potential litigation and product improvement) versus 3 years for EU customers.
- **Employee performance reviews** are retained for 7 years in the US (see the HR Data Retention Schedule). For EU employees, performance reviews are retained for 3 years after separation per the EU-specific override.

All other data categories follow the same retention periods in both jurisdictions.

## 4. State-Specific Requirements

Several US states impose additional retention or deletion obligations:

### 4.1 California (CCPA/CPRA)

- Acme Corp honors California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) rights for California residents.
- Consumers may request deletion of personal information. Acme Corp processes deletion requests within 45 days per CPRA (compared to 90 days for general customer deletion).
- Acme Corp does not sell personal information. The "Do Not Sell or Share" opt-out is available on the website but processes zero requests to date, as no sale occurs.

### 4.2 Virginia (VCDPA)

- Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act rights (access, correction, deletion, portability) are honored for Virginia residents.
- Response deadline: 45 days, with a 45-day extension if reasonably necessary.

### 4.3 Colorado (CPA)

- Colorado Privacy Act rights are honored for Colorado residents.
- Response deadline: 45 days, with a 45-day extension.

### 4.4 Other States

Acme Corp monitors the evolving US state privacy landscape. As of the effective date of this schedule, similar laws in Connecticut, Utah, Oregon, Texas, Montana, and Iowa are tracked by Legal. When a new state law takes effect, Legal updates this schedule within 90 days.

## 5. Litigation Hold Override

US litigation hold obligations supersede all retention schedules. When the General Counsel issues a litigation hold, affected records must be preserved regardless of whether they have reached the end of their retention period. Destruction of records subject to a litigation hold may constitute spoliation of evidence and expose the company to sanctions.

Employees who receive a litigation hold notice must confirm receipt and compliance within 48 hours.

## 6. Disposal

Records that have reached the end of their US retention period are deleted per the Data Retention Policy's Data Deletion Procedures (90 calendar days from expiration, verified by the Data Protection Officer). Physical records are destroyed using certified cross-cut shredding.

## 7. Policy Governance

This schedule is maintained by Legal. It is reviewed semi-annually to incorporate new state privacy laws. Questions should be directed to legal@acmecorp.com.
