Privacy Policy

Effective Date: November 1, 2024
Last Updated: November 1, 2024

Acme Corp ("we," "us," "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of individuals who visit our website, use our platform, or otherwise interact with our services. This Privacy Policy describes the personal data we collect, how we use it, how long we keep it, and the choices available to you.

1. Data We Collect

1.1 Data You Provide

  • Account information: Name, email address, company name, job title, phone number, and billing address provided during registration or account updates.
  • Payment information: Credit card number, billing address, and payment method details. Payment data is processed by our third-party payment processor and is not stored on Acme Corp servers.
  • Support requests: Information you provide when contacting support, including message content, attachments, and metadata.
  • Content you upload: Documents, files, and data you upload to the platform as part of your use of the service.

1.2 Data We Collect Automatically

  • Usage data: Pages visited, features used, actions taken, timestamps, session duration, and referral source.
  • Device and browser data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, and screen resolution.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: We use cookies, pixels, and local storage to recognize returning users, remember preferences, and analyze usage patterns. See our Cookie Policy for details.
  • Log data: Server logs that record requests made to our systems, including URL, IP address, response code, and timestamp.

1.3 Data from Third Parties

  • Single sign-on providers: If you log in through a third-party identity provider (Google Workspace, Okta, Azure AD), we receive your name, email, and profile picture.
  • Enrichment services: We may supplement account data with publicly available business information (company size, industry) from third-party data providers for sales and marketing purposes.

3. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data. We share personal data only in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers: Third-party vendors who process data on our behalf (hosting, payment processing, analytics, support tools). All service providers are bound by data processing agreements and may only use data for the purposes we specify.
  • Legal requirements: When required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, with notice to affected users.
  • With your consent: When you explicitly authorize sharing with a specific third party.

A list of our current sub-processors is available upon request by emailing privacy@acmecorp.com.

5. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Restriction: Request that we limit processing of your personal data under certain circumstances.
  • Data portability: Request a machine-readable copy of data you provided to us.
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interest, including profiling.
  • Withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@acmecorp.com. We respond to all requests within 30 days. If we need more time (up to 60 additional days for complex requests), we will notify you of the extension and the reason.

6. Opt-Out Procedures

6.1 Marketing Communications

You may opt out of marketing emails at any time by clicking the "unsubscribe" link in any marketing email, or by emailing privacy@acmecorp.com. Opt-out requests are processed within 5 business days. Transactional communications (security alerts, billing notices, service updates) are not affected by marketing opt-out.

6.2 Cookies

You can manage cookie preferences through the cookie consent banner displayed on your first visit, or at any time via the "Cookie Preferences" link in the website footer. See the Cookie Policy for details on specific cookies and their purposes.

6.3 Data Enrichment

To opt out of third-party data enrichment, email privacy@acmecorp.com with the subject line "Opt out of enrichment." We will remove enrichment data from your profile within 30 days.

7. International Data Transfers

Acme Corp is based in the United States. If you are located outside the U.S., your data may be transferred to and processed in the U.S. or other countries where our service providers operate. We protect international transfers using Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission, or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

EU customer data may be stored in our EU-West region upon request. See the Enterprise SLA for data residency options.

8. Security

We implement technical and organizational measures to protect personal data, including encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3), access controls, audit logging, and regular security assessments. For details, see our IT Security Policy. No system is perfectly secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security but we work to minimize risk.

9. Children's Privacy

Acme Corp's services are not directed to individuals under 16 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If we become aware that we have collected data from a child, we will delete it promptly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via email to account holders and by posting a notice on our website at least 30 days before the changes take effect. Your continued use of the service after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
