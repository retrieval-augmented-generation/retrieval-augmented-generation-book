## SOC 2 Type II

Acme Corp's SOC 2 Type II report covers a 12-month observation period and is issued annually. The March 2024 audit resulted in zero critical findings. Two advisory observations were noted:

1. One quarterly access review was completed 8 days late (remediated — see Audit Findings 2024, finding IT-01).
2. Documentation of a change management exception was incomplete (remediated within the observation period).

The SOC 2 report is available to customers and prospects under NDA. Requests should be directed to security@acmecorp.com.

### Trust Services Criteria Covered

- **Security:** Logical and physical access controls, network security, vulnerability management, incident response.
- **Availability:** System monitoring, disaster recovery, backup procedures, capacity planning.
- **Confidentiality:** Data classification, encryption, access restrictions, data retention and disposal.

Processing Integrity and Privacy criteria are not currently in scope. Addition of the Privacy criterion is under consideration for the 2026 audit cycle.

## ISO 27001

Acme Corp's ISO 27001 certification covers the Information Security Management System (ISMS) as defined in the Statement of Applicability (SoA). The certification was originally obtained in January 2022 and is valid for a 3-year cycle, with annual surveillance audits.

- **Initial certification:** January 2022
- **First recertification:** January 2024 (3-year renewal)
- **Next surveillance audit:** January 2025
- **Next recertification:** January 2027

The ISMS scope includes all production infrastructure, corporate IT systems, office facilities, and personnel security processes. The SoA maps 93 controls from ISO 27001:2022 Annex A to Acme Corp's internal policies and procedures.

## HIPAA

Acme Corp maintains HIPAA compliance for the subset of its platform that processes protected health information (PHI) on behalf of healthcare customers. HIPAA compliance is not a certification issued by a third party; it is maintained through:

- Annual risk assessment (most recent: April 2024).
- Implementation of administrative, physical, and technical safeguards as described in the HIPAA Compliance Overview.
- Executed Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all healthcare customers and sub-processors that handle PHI.
- Annual HIPAA training for employees with access to PHI (per the Mandatory Training Catalog).
- Incident response procedures aligned with the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (see the Incident Response Runbook, Section 4).

The risk assessment is conducted internally by the Information Security team and validated by external healthcare compliance counsel.
