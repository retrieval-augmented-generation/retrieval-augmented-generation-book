# Customer Success Playbook

**Effective Date:** November 1, 2024
**Owner:** VP of Customer Success
**Applies to:** All Customer Success Managers (CSMs) and Technical Account Managers (TAMs)

---

## 1. Customer Lifecycle

Every customer relationship follows four phases:

| Phase | Duration | CSM Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | First 30 days | Activation, setup, training |
| Adoption | Days 31–90 | Feature usage, workflow integration |
| Value realization | Days 91–renewal | Business outcomes, expansion |
| Renewal | 90 days before renewal date | Retention, upsell, contract negotiation |

## 2. Onboarding

### Self-Serve and Pro Accounts

- Automated welcome email with quickstart guide link (sent at provisioning).
- CSM outreach within 5 business days for accounts over 20 seats.
- 30-day check-in email (automated) with adoption metrics and suggested next steps.

### Enterprise Accounts

- Kickoff call within 5 business days of go-live (coordinated with Solutions Engineering if data migration is involved).
- Assign success plan: define 3 measurable outcomes the customer expects to achieve in the first 90 days.
- Weekly check-ins during the first 30 days, then biweekly through day 90.
- Admin training session delivered within 10 business days of provisioning.

## 3. Health Scoring

Each account is assigned a health score updated weekly:

| Signal | Weight | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Login frequency (DAU/MAU ratio) | 25% | Product analytics |
| Feature adoption breadth | 20% | Product analytics |
| Support ticket volume and severity | 20% | Support system |
| NPS / CSAT survey response | 15% | Survey tool |
| Contract utilization (seats used vs purchased) | 10% | Billing system |
| Executive sponsor engagement | 10% | CSM assessment |

### Health Categories

| Score | Category | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 80–100 | Healthy | Maintain cadence; explore expansion |
| 60–79 | Neutral | Investigate declining signals; schedule check-in |
| 40–59 | At risk | Escalate to CS leadership; develop recovery plan within 5 business days |
| Below 40 | Critical | Immediate outreach; executive sponsor engagement; weekly recovery meetings |

## 4. Renewals

CSMs begin renewal preparation 90 days before the contract end date:

| Timeline | Action |
|---|---|
| 90 days out | Review health score, usage trends, and open support tickets. Draft renewal proposal. |
| 60 days out | Schedule renewal meeting with customer stakeholder. Present value summary and renewal terms. |
| 45 days out | If expansion opportunity exists, present upsell proposal (additional seats, add-ons, tier upgrade). |
| 30 days out | Finalize terms. Escalate to VP of Customer Success if discount or non-standard terms are requested. |
| 15 days out | Contract sent for signature. |
| Renewal date | Contract executed or escalated to churn mitigation. |

## 5. Churn Mitigation

When a customer indicates intent to cancel or declines renewal:

1. **Understand the reason.** Conduct a candid conversation. Common reasons: budget cuts, low adoption, missing feature, competitive switch, internal reorganization.
2. **Assess recoverability.** Can the issue be addressed within the remaining contract period?
3. **Develop a save offer** (CSM + VP of CS approval required):
   - Temporary discount (up to 20% for one renewal cycle — VP approval).
   - Free add-on trial to demonstrate additional value.
   - Dedicated TAM engagement for 90 days.
   - Deferred payment terms (Finance approval required).
4. **Present the offer.** One conversation, one offer. Do not negotiate in stages.
5. **Document the outcome.** Win or lose, log the reason and outcome in the CRM for churn analysis.

Save offers exceeding 20% discount or involving contract restructuring require CFO approval.

## 6. Expansion

Expansion opportunities are identified through:

- Seat utilization above 90% (suggest adding seats).
- Feature adoption concentrated in one product line (suggest cross-sell to Data & Analytics or Compliance Suite).
- Customer requests for features available on a higher tier (suggest upgrade).
- Health score above 80 with executive sponsor engaged (ideal expansion candidate).

CSMs should present expansion proposals during quarterly business reviews, not during support interactions.

## 7. Escalation Path

| Situation | Escalate To |
|---|---|
| At-risk account (score < 60) | VP of Customer Success |
| Churn save offer > 20% discount | CFO (Lisa Yamamoto) |
| Technical issue blocking adoption | Engineering via TAM |
| Contract dispute or legal concern | Legal |
