# Hardware Request Procedure

**Effective Date:** September 15, 2024
**Applies to:** All Acme Corp employees

## 1. Eligible Hardware

Employees may request the following through this procedure:

- Laptops (new or replacement).
- External monitors (up to two per employee).
- Docking stations and USB-C hubs.
- Keyboards, mice, and other peripherals.
- Hardware upgrades (RAM, SSD) for existing company-issued devices, where supported by the model.
- Ergonomic equipment (standing desk converters, monitor arms) — coordinate with Facilities for desk-mounted items.

Mobile devices (phones, tablets) are requested through a separate process managed by the IT mobile team.

## 2. Request Process

1. **Submit a request** through the IT self-service portal at it-portal.acmecorp.com. Select "Hardware Request" and specify the item, business justification, and any urgency.
2. **Manager approval.** All hardware requests require direct manager approval. The manager receives an email notification and must approve or deny within 3 business days. Requests not approved within 5 business days are automatically closed.
3. **IT review.** After manager approval, IT Operations reviews the request for compatibility, inventory availability, and policy compliance. IT may suggest an alternative if the requested item is out of stock or not on the approved hardware list.
4. **Procurement.** If the item is in inventory, IT provisions and ships it. If the item must be ordered, IT places the order and provides an estimated delivery date.
5. **Delivery and setup.** Hardware is delivered to the employee's office location or shipped to their home address (for approved remote employees). IT pre-configures laptops with the standard image, disk encryption, and endpoint agent before delivery.

## 3. Delivery SLAs

| Item | In-Stock SLA | Out-of-Stock SLA |
|---|---|---|
| Laptop (standard model) | 3 business days | 10–15 business days |
| Monitor | 3 business days | 7–10 business days |
| Peripherals (keyboard, mouse, dock) | 2 business days | 5–7 business days |
| Hardware upgrade (RAM, SSD) | 5 business days (includes scheduling) | 10–15 business days |

SLAs are measured from the date of IT approval (not the date of the employee's initial request).

## 4. Budget

Standard hardware (laptops, monitors, peripherals) is funded by the IT budget and does not require department budget approval. Non-standard or premium hardware (e.g., high-end GPU workstations, ultrawide monitors) requires department budget approval in addition to manager approval.

The IT self-service portal indicates which items are standard and which require additional approval.

## 5. Returns and Replacements

- Defective hardware is replaced at no cost. Submit a support ticket to IT with the asset tag and a description of the issue.
- Hardware reaching end-of-life (typically 4 years for laptops, 5 years for monitors) is proactively replaced by IT. Employees are notified 30 days before the scheduled replacement.
- All returned hardware must be surrendered to IT for secure data wiping and asset tracking.

## 6. Policy Governance

This procedure is maintained by IT Operations. Questions should be directed to it-support@acmecorp.com or Slack #it-help.
