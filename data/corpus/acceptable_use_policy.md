# Acceptable Use Policy

**Document ID:** ITSEC-006
**Effective Date:** November 1, 2023
**Applies to:** All Acme Corp employees and contractors

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals who access Acme Corp systems, networks, or data, including full-time employees, part-time employees, temporary workers, interns, and contractors. The same standards apply regardless of employment classification. References to "users" throughout this document include all of these groups.

## 2. Permitted Use

Company-provided equipment, systems, and network access are provided for business purposes. Users may:

- Access company systems to perform their assigned job duties.
- Use company email and messaging for business communications.
- Use company devices for limited personal use (e.g., personal email during breaks, brief web browsing) provided it does not interfere with work responsibilities, consume excessive bandwidth, or violate any other provision of this policy.
- Install software from the approved software catalog. Software not in the catalog requires an IT exception request.

## 3. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are prohibited on company systems, networks, and devices:

- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content.
- Installing unauthorized software, including peer-to-peer file sharing applications, cryptocurrency mining software, or unapproved browser extensions.
- Attempting to bypass security controls, disable endpoint protection, or tamper with audit logging.
- Using company resources for personal commercial purposes (side businesses, freelance work, personal advertising).
- Sharing credentials, using another person's account, or allowing others to use your access.
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network without IT approval.
- Storing Confidential or Restricted data on personal cloud services (Google Drive personal, Dropbox personal, iCloud).
- Sending Confidential or Restricted data to personal email addresses.
- Excessive personal use that degrades system performance or bandwidth for other users.

## 4. Monitoring Disclosure

Acme Corp monitors the use of company systems, networks, and devices. Monitoring includes but is not limited to:

- Network traffic analysis (bandwidth usage, destination domains, protocol analysis).
- Email scanning for malware and data loss prevention (DLP) rule violations.
- Endpoint activity logging (application usage, file access on company devices).
- Access logs for company applications and cloud services.

Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company-provided systems and devices. Monitoring is conducted for security, compliance, and operational purposes. Monitoring data is reviewed by the Information Security team and is not used for general performance evaluation. Refer to the Employee Monitoring Policy for the full scope and governance of monitoring activities.

## 5. Internet and Social Media

- Web browsing for personal use during breaks is permitted. Accessing sites that are illegal, offensive, or that violate other company policies (harassment, discrimination) is prohibited at all times.
- Employees should not represent themselves as speaking on behalf of Acme Corp on social media unless authorized by the Communications team.
- Posting Confidential or Restricted company information on any public forum, social media platform, or personal blog is prohibited.

## 6. Mobile and BYOD

Personal devices used to access company systems must be enrolled in the Mobile Device Management (MDM) program per the BYOD Policy. Unenrolled personal devices may not connect to the corporate network or access company data.

## 7. Remote Work

Users accessing company systems remotely must use the Acme Corp VPN. See the VPN Access Guide for setup and connection instructions. All provisions of this policy apply equally when working remotely.

## 8. Consequences of Violation

Violations of this policy may result in:

- Temporary or permanent revocation of system access.
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination (for employees).
- Immediate contract termination (for contractors).
- Referral to law enforcement where illegal activity is suspected.

The severity of the response is determined by the nature and intent of the violation, whether it is a first or repeated offense, and the impact on company systems, data, or other users.

## 9. Reporting Violations

Users who become aware of a violation of this policy should report it to the Information Security team at security@acmecorp.com or through the anonymous ethics hotline at 1-800-555-0142.

## 10. Policy Governance

This policy is reviewed annually by the Information Security team. Questions should be directed to security@acmecorp.com.
